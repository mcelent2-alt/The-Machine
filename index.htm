Introduction

This is the documented, contemporaneous record of a single unbroken intellectual thread. Every entry is taken directly from primary sources: Mooers’ notebooks, patents, IEEE papers, ARPANET emails, conference proceedings, and corporate records. Nothing is paraphrased. Nothing is added after the fact.

The core idea is simple: A computer should behave like a nervous system — self-repairing, adaptive, distributed, and capable of genuine thinking. The same biological principles that govern human cognition must govern machine cognition.

Master Timeline (1945–2026)

Year Milestone Key Documents / Sources
1945–1946Calvin Mooers joins Naval Ordnance Lab computer project headed by John Atanasoff. Von Neumann is initiator and consultant. Mooers meets von Neumann; learns ENIAC/EDVAC plans, Turing’s 1936 paper, and Pitts-McCulloch neural symbolism for neural connections. Mooers notebooks record discussions on Turing Machine and nervous net. Atanasoff orders Mooers to more useful matters.Mooers notebooks & diary
1950Mooers coins the term “information retrieval” and founds Zator Company, the first information retrieval company.Zator Technical Bulletin 55
1951Mooers publishes Making Information Retrieval Pay and invents Zatocoding.Zator Technical Bulletin 55
1960sMooers invents TRAC language. Rockford Research Institute (founded by Mooers) seeks legal protection for TRAC to prevent alteration, modification, or extension. Bernard Galler debate in ACM Communications on intellectual property vs. user improvements. Mooers’ policy leads to limited adoption of TRAC.ACM Communications (Galler debate)
1978Command Technologies is founded by Franco Vitaliano and Calvin Mooers to market products melding neurology with Mooers’ inventions.Incorporation records
1981Franco Vitaliano (formerly Vitagliano) registers technical drawing or model titled “The Living Wall.”U.S. Copyright Office (reg_technical_drawing_or_model.csv)
1983Franco tries to commercialize TRAC-II with Calvin Mooers. Mooers reformulates TRAC methodology into TRAC-II. Franco catches attention but fails to sell. Mooers and Vitaliano part ways after several years. Mooers continues TRAC-II development and demonstrates document paging via Telnet at BBN (similar to later Gopher).Mooers interview & diary
1983Stanojević G. D. publishes “Language and limbic system” (Proc. 25th Congress of Yugoslavian Medical Students).National Conference Full Papers
1984Franco Vitaliano co-invents ergonomic handbar patent for tactile stimulation, visual stimulation, balance, relaxation, and safety during running. Abstract describes elongated polygonal article with neck, tapered length, roughened thumb area, and corner edges.USPTO 4,659,076 (Application 06809648, Grant 21 Apr 1987, IPC A63B 23/00, A61H 39/04)
1984Handbar classified under Human Necessities / Medical or Veterinary Science / Physical Therapy Apparatus (devices for locating or stimulating reflex points, e.g. shiatsu/acupuncture).USPTO classification documents
1985VXM Engine (“software robot”) publicly revealed. Command Technologies introduces VXM (Virtual Extended Machine) engine — culmination of Mooers’ macrogenerator work. Installs machine-dependent version on each target node; common front end converts commands to local OS commands.Computerworld article (John Dix)
1985Command Technologies described as creating a computer system “Like A Nervous System.” Crosses computer luminary’s research with clinical psychologist’s knowledge.Contemporary press clipping
1985Franco Vitaliano advertises high-performance 133 Megabyte Winchester Disk Drives for IBM AT/PC via S/COM Systems.FOR IMMEDIATE SALE advertisement
1986Mooers presents IEEE paper: “Use of transportable scripts and VXM agents for uniform command in a mixed hardware environment.”IEEE Int. Conf. Syst., Man. and Cybern., Atlanta, Vol. 1, pp. 737-741
1986Burroughs Corp. & Sperry Corp. merger brings Command Technologies “out of the woodwork.” Company’s first product allows Burroughs mainframe to operate with Sperry mainframe using artificial intelligence to reformat command structures between operating systems.Software Notes column
1986VXM Entity concept published: semiautonomous module of code that can reside on any network node or travel between nodes like a “ghost” in the machine. Communicates via ASCII; controls non-VXM programs.Mini-Micro Systems article
1987Stanojević G. D. publishes multiple papers on integrative psychotherapeutic approach and hypnosis for sexual dysfunctions (2 case studies).Yugoslavian Psychotherapists’ Publication: Psychotherapy and Human Sexuality, Vol. 2
1987Stanojević G. D. presents “Hypnosis in integrative psychotherapy” and “Application of hypnosis with patients themselves requiring hypnotherapy” at 5th Yugoslavian Congress of Psychotherapists.National Conference Full Papers
1987SoftRobot: world’s first self-repairing agent. BusinessWeek describes software robot that autonomously repairs networked systems while running. Can move data on hard-disk crash, restart drives, fool system into thinking a person is working. Cost ~$2,500 for workstation systems. Evaluated by a dozen computer companies.BusinessWeek (Sep 7, 1987, p. 113)
1987ARPANET email from Maj. Doug Hardie forwards BusinessWeek SoftRobot article describing on-line robotic repair of software.Command Technologies email (Hardie@DOCKMASTER.ARPA, 15 Sep 87)
1988Franco Vitaliano publishes “Upper Layer Interoperability” in ConneXions: The Interoperability Report. Discusses TCP/IP vs. OSI transition, NFS, RPC, APPC vs. Named Pipes, distributed operating systems (CRONUS), SAA, distributed databases, SQL. Introduces TIM (session layer for TCP/IP) from VXM Technologies as bridge.ConneXions: The Interoperability Report, v. 2, no. 10, pp. 6-10 (Oct 1988)
1988MITRE + USAF deploy VXM at Hanscom AFB for distributed command & control. Mitre uses VXM Network Shell to develop session- and application-layer services over heterogeneous TCP/IP network. Replaces custom code and Sun RPCs; interpretive language with uniform data structures; no recompiles needed when porting.Data Communications magazine (Newsfront section)
